📊 What are High Volume Candles?

A High Volume Candle (HVC) is a daily bar where the trading volume is at least 3x the 20-day average volume. These "skyscraper" bars signal that institutional money is entering a position — often marking the start of a major move before the price breakout is obvious.

Bull vs Bear Candles

Green = close > open (bullish). Red = close < open (bearish). Bull HVCs with gap-ups are the highest-conviction breakout signals.

Volume Ratio

How many times above the 20-day average. Higher = more unusual = stronger signal. 5x+ ratios are "extreme" and highlighted differently.

GAP

Gap Analysis

Gap Up = open above previous bar's high. Gap Down = open below previous bar's low. "Open" gaps haven't been filled yet — unfilled gaps often act as future support/resistance.

Price Change Tracking

The AEVA Example

AEVA triggered massive volume bars in April-May while the stock was still under $5. Those "skyscraper" bars flagged institutional accumulation months before the move to $30+. This scanner helps you catch those signals early across the entire Russell 3000 universe.
