📊 Relative Strength Rating

What is RS Rating?

The IBD-style Relative Strength Rating ranks every stock in the universe from 1 to 99 based on its price performance vs SPY over the past year. A stock with RS 99 has outperformed 99% of all stocks.

The Formula

RS = 0.4 × Q1 + 0.2 × Q2 + 0.2 × Q3 + 0.2 × Q4
Q1 = last 3 months, Q2 = 6 months, Q3 = 9 months, Q4 = 12 months
Each quarter is the ratio close/close[N] for stock vs SPY

Key Concepts

Monsters (RS ≥ 90) — Top decile stocks with exceptional relative strength. These are the stocks Stan Weinstein calls "monster stocks."

RS Breakout — RS hitting a 52-week high BEFORE price hits a new high. This is the strongest leading indicator — relative strength leads price.

RS 30WMA — The RS line's 30-week moving average. RS above its 30WMA = strengthening trend. Below = weakening.

Rate of Change — How many RS points a stock gained/lost over 5 or 20 trading days. Rapidly rising RS = accumulation.

Universe

Rankings use the full Russell 3000 (~2,944 stocks) as the comparison universe. This gives the truest picture of relative strength across the entire US equity market, including small caps where monster stocks often start.
